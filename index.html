SELF-REGULATION

Sustaining goal-directed behavior by controlling impulses, focusing attention and regulating emotions under pressure or distraction.

1. Sense-Making

Constructs a coherent model of reality by integrating metacognitive data with allocentric perspectives, reconciling their conflicts, and revising priors when warranted.

1a. Critical Thinking

Evaluates an assertion by estimating the plausibility of its proposition, assessing the reliability of its source, and stress-testing its justification for logical integrity and evidential relevance.

1b. Objectivity

Identifies uneven standards in belief formation, then applies empirical and rational criteria uniformly, to judge information accurately and fairly.

  • Example: Applies the “Veil of ignorance”

1c. Fairness

Applies impartial balance between empathy and norms of accountability in reciprocal interactions, irrespective of circumstances.

  • Example: Admits mistakes to prevent double standards and avoid hypocrisy

1d. Self-Reflection

Analyzes past mistakes to identify patterns, then formulates if/then strategies for similar future situations, avoiding rumination by converting reflection into actionable learning.

1e. Self-Efficacy

Emerges when confidence is grounded in an accurate assessment of one’s abilities, but it wanes if skills are unused or untested.

  • Achievement: Integrates personal achievements into self-concept
  • Perspective: Interprets setbacks as skill gaps rather than self-worth deficits

2. Self-Discipline

Applies self-regulation consistently to align behavior with long-term goals and principles, while avoiding rigidity and complacency.

2a. Perseverance

Sustains effort and endurance amid hardship or challenge by countering fear-based avoidance via commitment to a goal.

  • Mechanisms:
    • Treats fear-responses as trainable signals
    • Uses graduated exposure to fear to build resilience

2b. Temperance

Treats willpower as a limited resource and therefore allocates effort by order of importance.

  • Example: Compresses emotions into a single word to regain focus
