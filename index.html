Framework

Sense-Making

Constructs a coherent model of reality by reconciling any conflicts between your metacognitive data with allocentric perspectives.

Critical Thinking

Evaluates each assertion by estimating the plausibility of its proposition, assessing the credibility of its source, and stress-testing for evidential relevance.

Objectivity

Identifies uneven standards in belief formation, then applies empirical and rational criteria uniformly, to judge information impartially and fairly.

Example:
  • Applies the “Veil of ignorance”

Fairness

Contextually weighs between a person's empathy and the consequences of his actions.

Grounded Self-Efficacy

Emerges when confidence is grounded in an accurate assessment of one’s abilities, and confidence reflects one’s proven competence.

Examples:
  • Integrates personal achievements into self-concept
  • Interprets setbacks as skill gaps rather than personal deficits

Perseverance

Sustains effort and endurance by countering fear-induced avoidance with courage.

Example:
  • Uses graduated exposure to fear, which helps build resilience

Internal Locus of Control

Treats mental energy as a limited resource and in response it allocates effort to future events that can be influenced.
